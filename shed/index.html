Step-by-Step Construction Guide

Follow these phases in order. Click each phase to expand the detailed steps.

1. Clear & Mark: Clear a 6x18 foot area. Drive stakes to mark the four corners of a perfect 4x16 foot rectangle.

2. Run String Lines: Tightly run string between the stakes to create a visual outline of your foundation's perimeter.

3. Square the Frame (Critical Step): Measure the two diagonals of your string rectangle (from corner to corner). Adjust the stakes until the diagonal measurements are exactly the same. This guarantees a square foundation.

4. Lay Weed Barrier: Roll out landscape fabric over the entire area, going under your string lines. Secure it with landscape staples.

1. Mark Block Locations: Along your 16-foot string lines, mark the ground for your 10 Tuff Blocks (5 per side), with one at each corner and the rest spaced about 4 feet apart.

2. Dig Pads: At each mark, dig a shallow, flat-bottomed hole about 12"x12" and 4" deep.

3. Fill & Tamp: Pour 1-2 bags of paver base into each hole. Vigorously compact the gravel with a hand tamper until it's rock solid and level.

4. Place & Level Blocks: Position a Tuff Block on each pad. Use your 4-foot level to ensure each block is level. Use the level and a long, straight 2x4 to check that adjacent blocks are level with each other. Adjust gravel as needed.

1. Cut & Place Posts: Place a long piece of 4x4 into each of the 10 Tuff Blocks. Do not worry about the height yet.

2. Position Beams: Lay your two 16-foot 2x8 beams on top of the posts along each long side.

3. Level the Frame (Most Critical Step): Start at the post on the HIGHEST ground. Cut it to your desired height. Place the beam on it and use your level to find the correct height for the next post. Mark, remove, and cut that post. Repeat for all 10 posts until both beams are perfectly level and parallel.

4. Secure Beams: Attach the beams to the posts by driving two 3" structural screws down through the top of the beam into the center of each 4x4 post.

1. Mark Joist Locations: On the inside face of the beams, mark locations for interior joists every 16 inches on center.

2. Install Joist Hangers: Align the bottom of a joist hanger with the bottom of the beam at each mark. Nail them securely with 1 1/2" joist hanger nails, filling all available holes.

3. Install Interior Joists: Drop your 45-inch 2x8 joists into the hangers and secure them with hanger nails.

4. Install Plywood Decking: Lay your two 4x8 sheets of plywood across the frame, perpendicular to the interior joists. The seam between sheets should land on the center of a joist.

5. Secure Decking: Fasten the plywood with 2" decking screws every 6 inches along the perimeter and every 12 inches in the center.

1. Position Anchors: At the four corners of your foundation, screw the auger-style ground anchors into the ground just inside the frame.

2. Attach Straps: Securely fasten a strap or steel cable from each anchor to the wooden frame (beam or outer joist). This locks your foundation in place and satisfies MN code requirements.
