Step-by-Step Construction Guide

Follow these phases in order. Click each phase to expand the detailed steps.

1. Clear & Mark: Clear a 6x18 foot area. Drive stakes to mark the four corners of a perfect 4x16 foot rectangle.

2. Run String Lines: Tightly run string between the stakes to create a visual outline of your foundation's perimeter.

3. Square the Frame (Critical Step): Measure the two diagonals of your string rectangle (from corner to corner). Adjust the stakes until the diagonal measurements are exactly the same. This guarantees a square foundation.

4. Lay Weed Barrier: Roll out landscape fabric over the entire area, going under your string lines. Secure it with landscape staples.

1. Mark Block Locations: Along your 16-foot string lines, mark the ground for your 10 Tuff Blocks (5 per side), with one at each corner and the rest spaced about 4 feet apart.

2. Clear Sod & Level Area: At each of the 10 marks, clear all grass and organic material down to the bare soil in a 12"x12" area. Ensure the ground is flat and level.

3. Create Gravel Pad: Add a 1-2 inch layer of paver base onto the cleared soil. Vigorously compact the gravel with a hand tamper until it's rock solid and perfectly level.

4. Place & Level Blocks: Position a Tuff Block on each compacted pad. Use your 4-foot level to ensure each block is level. Use the level and a long, straight 2x4 to check that adjacent blocks are level with each other. Adjust gravel as needed.

1. Place Posts & Beams: Place a long 4x4 into each Tuff Block. Rest the two 16-foot 2x8 beams on top of the posts.

2. Level & Mark for Notches (Most Critical Step): Start at the post on the HIGHEST ground. Level the beam and mark BOTH the top and bottom of the beam on the side of the post. Repeat for all 10 posts. Each post will have a unique set of marks.

3. Cut the Notches: Remove a post. Set your circular saw depth to 1.5 inches (the thickness of a 2x8). Make multiple passes inside your marks. Clean out the waste with a hammer and finish the cut square with a hand saw. The goal is a flat "shelf" for the beam to sit in.

4. Seal Cuts (Essential): Apply end-cut wood preservative to all freshly cut surfaces on your posts. Let it dry according to the product's instructions.

5. Seat & Secure Beams: Place the posts back in the Tuff Blocks and seat the beams into the fresh notches. Secure by driving two 3" structural screws through the side of the beam into the post for each notch.

1. Install End Joists: Place the two end joists flush with the ends of the beams. Attach them by driving 3" structural screws through the outside of the 16' beams into the ends of these joists (3-4 screws per connection).

2. Mark for Interior Joists: On the inside face of the beams, mark locations for the 5 remaining interior joists every 16 inches on center.

3. Install Joist Hangers & Joists: Attach joist hangers at the marks and install the interior joists using 1 1/2" hanger nails.

4. Install Plywood Decking: Lay your two 4x8 sheets of plywood across the frame, perpendicular to the interior joists. The seam between sheets should land on the center of a joist.

5. Secure Decking: Fasten the plywood with 2" decking screws every 6 inches along the perimeter and every 12 inches in the center.

1. Position Anchors: At the four corners of your foundation, screw the auger-style ground anchors into the ground just inside the frame.

2. Attach Straps: Securely fasten a strap or steel cable from each anchor to the wooden frame (beam or outer joist). This locks your foundation in place and satisfies MN code requirements.
